AI Use Statement

Responsible Use of
AI in Content Development

How the Mohsin Haematology Academy uses AI-assisted tools to support educational and clinical decision-support material, and the human review and verification standards that sit alongside them.

Important: AI tools do not replace specialist clinical judgement, MDT discussion, peer review, or local governance approval. All AI-assisted output is treated as a draft until verified by the clinical author against recognised sources.

Scope
How AI-assisted tools are used

Where AI assistance is applied

Mohsin Haematology Academy may use AI-assisted tools to support drafting, structuring, editing, formatting, evidence organisation, and technical implementation of educational and clinical decision-support materials.

Human author review

All clinical content remains subject to human author review. AI-assisted output is not treated as authoritative without clinician verification.

Verification standards

Clinical recommendations, drug access statements, references, and governance-sensitive wording must be checked against recognised sources, including current NICE guidance, NHS England commissioning positions, BSH guidance, SmPCs, and local Trust policy where relevant.

What AI does not replace

AI tools do not replace specialist clinical judgement, MDT discussion, peer review, or local governance approval.

Author responsibility: The clinical author retains full responsibility for all published content. AI-assisted drafting is a productivity aid, not a substitute for clinical accountability. If you identify content that appears inaccurate or out of date, please contact us via the Contact page.
